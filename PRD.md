# PRD: Studio 14 Website and Telegram Assistant

## 1. Context

Studio 14 is an acting studio in Lisbon. The studio runs several educational and event-based projects in parallel: entry courses for new Studio 14 students, continuing courses for Studio 14 alumni, workshops with invited teachers, and potentially other studio events.

The first product goal is to create a clear public-facing website that explains what Studio 14 is, presents current programs, and routes interested people to Telegram for questions and registration.

The second product goal is to create a Telegram assistant that can answer common questions, explain the available programs, and help guide people toward the right next step.

## 2. Goals

### Website Goals

- Present Studio 14 as a credible, active acting studio in Lisbon.
- Explain the studio's approach, atmosphere, and value for students.
- Show several active programs at the same time without making the website feel overloaded.
- Give each program a clear entry point and a clear Telegram call to action.
- Make it easy to add, remove, or update programs and events later.

### Telegram Assistant Goals

- Answer common questions from prospective students.
- Explain the differences between programs.
- Help users choose the most relevant course or workshop based on their experience and goals.
- Collect useful registration or lead information when appropriate.
- Escalate to a human contact when the question is too specific or requires manual confirmation.

## 3. Target Audience

The first launch is focused only on a Russian-speaking audience in Lisbon. The primary audience is Russian-speaking people who are interested in acting, performance, self-expression, stage presence, cinema, theatre, or creative practice.

Potential audience segments:

- New Studio 14 students who have not studied at the studio before. Their acting experience outside Studio 14 does not matter for the first course.
- Studio 14 alumni who have already completed the "База" course and want to continue developing.
- Creative professionals who want to improve presence, confidence, voice, movement, or improvisation.
- People interested in short-format workshops with invited international teachers.
- Russian-speaking residents of Lisbon and nearby areas.

## 4. Product Scope

### Included in the First Version

- A main Studio 14 landing page.
- A structured list or feed of current programs and events.
- Dedicated program pages or detailed program sections for:
  - "База" course for people who are new to Studio 14.
  - Workshop with a professor from Fullerton, Los Angeles.
  - Continuing course for students who have already completed "База".
- A dedicated page or section about the Studio 14 founders and course leaders.
- Telegram buttons on the main page and inside each program direction.
- A Telegram assistant concept that can later be implemented as a bot.

### Not Yet Defined

- Exact course dates, prices, duration, location, and teacher bios.
- Whether program details should live on separate pages or in a feed/card format.
- Whether Telegram should lead to a human chat, a group/channel, or a bot-first flow.
- Payment, booking, and CRM integration.

## 5. Website Structure

### Recommended Structure for First Version

The website should have one main landing page with sections for the studio, current programs, and contact. Each program can initially be presented as a detailed section or card with a dedicated call to action.

If program content becomes large, each card can link to a separate program page. This keeps the first version simple while leaving room to grow.

### Main Landing Page Sections

1. Hero section
   - Studio 14 name.
   - Short positioning statement.
   - Primary Telegram call to action.
   - Secondary link to current programs.

2. About Studio 14
   - What the studio is.
   - Who it is for.
   - What kind of acting practice or method the studio offers.
   - Why Lisbon is part of the studio identity.

3. Current Programs
   - "База" course.
   - Fullerton / Los Angeles professor workshop.
   - Continuing course.
   - Future events can be added to the same structure.

4. Studio Approach
   - A concise explanation of the teaching philosophy.
   - This section should only stay if it adds clarity. If it feels abstract or repetitive, it can be merged into the About section.

5. Teachers / Guests
   - Studio 14 founders and course leaders.
   - Invited professor or guest teacher for the workshop.

6. FAQ
   - Who can join?
   - What language are classes in?
   - Do I need acting experience, or is "База" required because I am new to Studio 14?
   - Where are classes held?
   - How do I register?

7. Contact / Telegram
   - Clear final call to action.
   - Telegram button.
   - Optional Instagram or email link.

## 6. Program Presentation

Each program should have a consistent content structure:

- Program name.
- Short description.
- Who it is for.
- What participants will work on.
- Format: course, workshop, intensive, weekly classes, etc.
- Dates and duration.
- Language.
- Location.
- Teacher.
- Price, if ready to publish.
- Telegram button for questions and registration.

### Program 1: "База" Course

Purpose:
Introduce people who are new to Studio 14 to the studio's practice, language, atmosphere, and working principles.

Audience:
People who have never studied at Studio 14 before. Prior acting experience outside the studio does not matter: a person can be completely new to acting or already have some experience, but if they are new to Studio 14, "База" is the entry point.

Draft positioning:
"База" is the first Studio 14 course for everyone who is entering the studio for the first time. It introduces the core tools, principles, and shared language of the studio: attention, body, voice, imagination, partner work, and scene work.

### Program 2: Workshop With Professor From Fullerton, Los Angeles

Purpose:
Create a special event around an invited international teacher.

Audience:
Students with interest in a focused workshop, actors, performers, and people who want to experience another professional teaching perspective.

Draft positioning:
A limited-format workshop with an invited professor from Fullerton, Los Angeles, focused on professional acting practice and direct work with participants.

### Program 3: Continuing Course

Purpose:
Offer a next step for people who have already completed the Studio 14 "База" course.

Audience:
Studio 14 students who have already studied on "База" and want deeper practice beyond the entry level.

Draft positioning:
For Studio 14 alumni who want to continue developing acting technique, work more deeply with scenes and partners, and build consistency in practice.

- Show the acting, film, television, and teaching background of the course leaders.
- Give prospective students a human reason to trust the studio.

## 8. Telegram Assistant

The Telegram assistant should act as the first point of contact for people interested in Studio 14.

### Core Responsibilities

- Greet new users and ask what they are interested in.
- Present the available programs.
- Explain the difference between "База", continuing courses, and workshop formats.
- Answer FAQ-style questions.
- Ask clarifying questions when the user is unsure which program fits them.
- Offer a direct path to register or contact a human.

### Suggested Bot Flow

1. User opens Telegram from the website.
2. Assistant asks what the user is interested in:
   - "База".
   - Continuing course.
   - Workshop.
   - Not sure yet.
   - Talk to a person.
3. Assistant gives a concise explanation of the selected option.
4. Assistant answers questions or collects registration interest.
5. Assistant either:
   - Sends the user to a human contact.
   - Records lead information.
   - Shares next steps, dates, price, and location.

### Information the Assistant May Collect

- Name.
- Acting experience level.
- Program of interest.
- Telegram username or contact.
- Availability.
- Whether the user wants a human follow-up.

### Escalation to Human

The assistant should escalate when:

- The user asks about discounts, exceptions, or payment issues.
- The user asks a personal or sensitive question.
- The user wants to reserve a place.
- The bot does not have the information needed to answer accurately.

## 9. Content Tone

The website and bot should feel:

- Clear.
- Warm.
- Professional.
- Human.
- Not too corporate.
- Not too theatrical or vague.

All first-version public copy should be written in Russian. The copy should avoid abstract promises and focus on concrete experience: practice, classes, teachers, format, community, and next steps.

## 10. Open Questions

- Should the first version be one long landing page, separate pages per program, or a hybrid?
- Should Telegram lead to a bot, a human account, a group, or a channel?
- What is the exact name and bio of the Fullerton professor?
- What are the dates, prices, duration, and schedule for each program?
- Should Studio 14 position itself more around theatre, cinema, actor training, self-expression, or creative community?
- Should the website include student photos, rehearsal photos, or video?
- How should the founder bios balance acting filmography, teaching experience, and the story of creating Studio 14?

## 11. Draft Landing Page Message

Studio 14 is an acting studio in Lisbon for Russian-speaking people who want to explore acting through live practice, attention, body, voice, imagination, and work with a partner.

The studio runs several programs at the same time: "База" for everyone who is new to Studio 14, continuing courses for students who have already completed "База", and special workshops with invited teachers. Each program has its own rhythm and level, but all of them are built around practical work and direct contact with the craft.

If you are not sure which format fits you, write to us in Telegram. We will help you choose the right next step.
